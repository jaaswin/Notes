CV can mean different things depending on the field. Since you are from an electronics and robotics background, let’s unwrap the most common technical meaning first.


---

1️⃣ Computer Vision (CV)

Computer Vision is a field of Artificial Intelligence that enables machines to “see” and understand images or videos.

If Machine Learning is the brain, Computer Vision is the artificial eye 👁️🤖

It allows systems to:

Detect objects

Recognize faces

Read text from images

Track motion

Understand scenes



---

How Computer Vision Works

A simple pipeline looks like this:

1. Image acquisition (Camera captures image)


2. Preprocessing (Remove noise, resize, enhance contrast)


3. Feature extraction (Edges, shapes, textures)


4. Model training (Usually Deep Learning)


5. Prediction / Decision



Most modern CV systems use Deep Learning models such as:

CNN (Convolutional Neural Networks)

YOLO (You Only Look Once)

R-CNN


For example:

Face recognition in smartphones

Self-driving cars detecting pedestrians

Industrial defect detection


Companies like Tesla use Computer Vision in autonomous driving systems.


---

Applications of Computer Vision

Robotics navigation

Medical image analysis

Surveillance systems

OCR systems

Gesture control

Smart agriculture

2️⃣ Curriculum Vitae (CV)

In another context, CV means Curriculum Vitae, a document that describes:

Education

---

3️⃣ Control Voltage (Electronics)

In electronics, CV can also mean Control Voltage, used in:

Oscillators

Synthesizers

Amplifiers


It is a voltage signal that controls a circuit parameter.
