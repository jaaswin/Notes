## üîå Network Theory in Electrical Engineering

In electrical and electronics engineering, network theory focuses on analyzing circuits made of interconnected components like:

* Resistors
* Inductors
* Capacitors
* Voltage sources
* Current sources

Here, a **network** means a combination of electrical elements connected together. When that network has at least one closed loop allowing current to flow, we call it a **circuit**.

Network theory provides the mathematical tools to analyze:

* Voltage distribution
* Current flow
* Power transfer
* Energy storage
* System stability

It turns a messy web of wires into clean equations.

## üß† Core Concepts of Network Theory

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ Nodes and Branches

A **node** is a junction where two or more elements meet.
A **branch** is a single element between two nodes.

Imagine cities and roads. Cities are nodes. Roads are branches. Electricity travels the roads.

---

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ Kirchhoff‚Äôs Laws

These are the backbone of network theory.

**Kirchhoff‚Äôs Current Law (KCL)**
At any node:
Sum of incoming currents = Sum of outgoing currents

Current does not vanish. It simply chooses different paths.

**Kirchhoff‚Äôs Voltage Law (KVL)**
Around any closed loop:
Sum of voltages = 0

Energy supplied equals energy consumed.

These two laws alone can solve most circuit problems.

### 4Ô∏è‚É£ Network Theorems

To simplify complex circuits, network theory provides elegant shortcuts:

* **Thevenin‚Äôs Theorem** ‚Üí Replace a complex network with a single voltage source and resistance
* **Norton‚Äôs Theorem** ‚Üí Replace it with a current source and resistance
* **Superposition Theorem** ‚Üí Analyze one source at a time
* **Maximum Power Transfer Theorem** ‚Üí Maximum power is delivered when load resistance equals source resistance

These theorems are like zooming out to see the whole forest instead of counting each tree.
