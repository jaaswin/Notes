Passive elements are the **quiet workers** of a circuit. They do not create energy, they do not amplify signals, and they never steal the spotlight. Instead, they **shape, store, limit, or release energy** that already exists. Think of them as the architecture of a building rather than the electricity inside it.

---

### What makes an element â€œpassiveâ€?

An element is called **passive** if it:

* Does **not require an external power source**
* Cannot **amplify** a signal
* Only **stores, dissipates, or transfers energy**

They obey the circuit faithfully, no drama involved.
